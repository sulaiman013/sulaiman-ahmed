Smoothing Explained
How Fabric Absorbs Your CU Spikes
Think of smoothing like a credit card bill. You can spend more than your limit in a single moment, as long as your average stays under the limit over the billing period.

Spike Smoothed over 5-64 min
Scenario
Monday 9 AM: 15 users open Power BI dashboards at the same time. The DAX queries spike to 20 CUs for 10 seconds.
Total consumption: 20 CUs × 10 sec = 200 CU-seconds.
Without smoothing: 20 CU peak hit. You'd need F32.
With 5-min smoothing: 200 ÷ 300 sec = only 0.67 CU average. Your F4 handles it fine.
Background Operations
Notebooks, dbt, Pipelines
24 hours
Fixed smoothing window
Capacity Limit
Spike Smoothed over 24 hours
Scenario
2 AM: You schedule a dbt job that uses 16 CUs for 30 minutes.
Total consumption: 16 CUs × 30 min = 480 CU-minutes.
Without smoothing: you'd need F16 capacity to avoid throttling.
With 24h smoothing: 480 ÷ 1,440 min in a day = only 0.33 CU average. Even an F2 handles this.

CU-Minutes Budget / Day
With an F4, you get 5,760 CU-minutes per day for background jobs. That means you could run:
  • A Notebook using 48 CUs for 2 hours (48 x 120 = 5,760 CU-min). It runs at full speed, no throttling.
  • Or 6 dbt jobs, each using 8 CUs for 2 hours (6 x 8 x 120 = 5,760 CU-min).
  • Or 12 Pipeline runs plus 3 Notebook jobs during off-peak hours.
The key: schedule heavy jobs at night. They borrow from idle hours and stay under the 24h budget.
