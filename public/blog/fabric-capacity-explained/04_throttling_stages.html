Progressive Throttling
What Happens When You Push Too Far
When your total CU usage exceeds what your SKU provides, Fabric does not instantly shut you down. Instead, it applies 4 progressive stages, giving you time to react.
Under limit
10-60 min over
1-24 hr over
24 hr+ over
✓
Safe Zone
≤ 10 min of future capacity
No throttling at all
You have an F4 (4 CUs). A Notebook spikes to 6 CUs for 5 minutes. You "borrowed" 2 extra CUs × 5 min = 10 CU-min of debt. That's under the 10-min buffer, so everything keeps running normally.
⏳
Delay
10 to 60 min overage
+20 sec delay on interactive
Same F4. Your debt grows to 30 minutes worth. A user opens a Power BI report, and it takes 20 extra seconds to load. Background jobs like dbt and Notebooks are completely unaffected.
⚠
Int. Rejection
1 hr to 24 hr overage
Interactive jobs blocked
Your debt hits 2 hours. Users try to open Power BI reports and get error messages. But your 2 AM dbt job still runs, and your Notebook pipelines keep executing in the background.
❌
Full Rejection
> 24 hours overage
Everything stops
Your debt exceeds 24 hours. No reports, no notebooks, no pipelines, nothing runs. You must either wait for the debt to clear, scale up the SKU, or pause and resume the capacity.
⚡ Special Throttling Exceptions
Real-Time Intelligence
Skips the 20-sec delay stage. Goes straight from safe to rejection. Why? Because a 20-second delay on real-time data defeats the purpose. It's either fast or blocked.
Eventstreams
Never fully rejected. Instead, Fabric reduces the CU resources powering the stream. Your stream stays alive but processes slower, like reducing from 100 events/sec to 20 events/sec.
Data Warehouse
Most queries are classified as Background with 24h smoothing. A 10-minute heavy query gets averaged across the full day, so it rarely triggers throttling at all.
Key Takeaway: Background jobs (Notebooks, dbt, Pipelines) survive Stages 1, 2, and 3. They only get blocked at Stage 4 (24h+ overage), which is rare. Schedule heavy workloads at night, and they run while your users sleep. By morning, the debt clears, and interactive reports work fine.
Source: Microsoft Learn, Fabric Throttling (Updated Feb 2026)
